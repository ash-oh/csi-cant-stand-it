<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Congratulations, Nicky, my boy. You are now a CSI Level Three.</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: (excited) Whoo!</p>
<p>[INT. CATHERINE'S SISTER'S HOUSE - BEDROOM - MORNING]</p>
<p>(LINDSEY wakes up.)</p>
<p><strong>Lindsey Willows</strong>: Mommy?</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: I couldn't drive here fast enough to tell you how much I love you.</p>
<p><strong>Lindsey Willows</strong>: I love you, too.</p>
<p>[Scenes from 1X02: Cool Change]</p>
<p>[INT. CSI - LAB]</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Sara Sidle?</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: (smiles) I know who I am. I think you're a little confused.</p>
<p>[INT. CSI - BRASS' OFFICE - DAY]</p>
<p>(GRISSOM checks into BRASS' office as he clears his stuff out.)</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: I just off the phone with the Sheriff. He says he wants me to run the unit.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: You're the boss.</p>
<p>[INT. CSI - HALLWAY]</p>
<p>(GRISSOM and WARRICK sit on the bench in the hallway.)</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: They say I got to let you go.</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: I know.</p>
<p>(GRISSOM stands and moves in front of WARRICK.)</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: I don't care what the book says. I lost one good person today, I don't want to lose another. </p>
<p>(GRISSOM holds out WARRICK'S gun and badge back out to him.)</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Here.</p>
<p>(WARRICK looks up at GRISSOM.)</p>
<p>CUT TO END OF &quot;PREVIOUSLY ON&quot;</p>
<p>FLASH IN.</p>
<p>[EXT. VARIOUS NEVADA DESERT (STOCK) - DAY]</p>
<p>(Camera close up of a shovel dropping dry dirt onto the ground and patting it down. A large booted foot steps onto the spot.)</p>
<p>(The MAN finishes and walks away from the spot with his shovel.)</p>
<p>HARD CUT TO BLACK.</p>
<p>(A lighter flame flickers on. The woman holding the lighter is lying down in the dark. She looks around at the enclosed space around her. She starts to panic and cry.)</p>
<p>(She screams as she realizes that she's inside a box.)</p>
<p>FADE TO BLACK.</p>
<hr />
[INT. GARRIS RESIDENCE -- NIGHT]</p>
<p>(Scene opens on the tape machine playing the ransom note. GRISSOM, NICK and JACK GARRIS listen to the message.)</p>
<p><strong>Altered Voice</strong>: (o.s.) (from tape) Bring $2 million in hundreds to Charleston and Third in three hours or your wife dies. (beat) Stop me from taking the money -- she still dies.</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: This guy thinks it's some kind of joke using a voice like that.</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: He's altering his voice, sir, electronically.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: We don't consider it a joke at all.</p>
<p>(GRISOM starts the tape over again.)</p>
<p><strong>Altered Voice</strong>: (from tape) Bring $2 million in hundreds ... </p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: (irritated and impatient) You guys have listened to that tape like, twenty times. Will somebody please go out and find my wife?</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Mr. Garris, please. Sometimes, if you listen closely you can hear a lot.</p>
<p>(GRISSOM rewinds the tape again. He plays it and everyone falls silent as they listen to the low frequency buzz of the player.)</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: (after a moment) I don't hear anything.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Right. Where do you hear nothing in Las Vegas?</p>
<p>(JACK GARRIS doesn't say anything as he thinks about it. GRISSOM answers his own question.)</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: The desert. (beat) And, judging by what sounds like a low frequency buzz in the background, possibly near power lines.</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: How'd you hear all that?</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: I listen.</p>
<p>(JACK GARRIS stares at GRISSOM and nods his head slightly.)</p>
<p>(In the background, we can hear someone talking on a phone.)</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: (to phone) Right, okay, all right, got it.</p>
<p>[INT. GARRIS RESIDENCE -- CONTINUOUS]</p>
<p>(BRASS makes his way across the living room toward them. He puts his phone away.)</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: (to GRISSOM) I just spoke with the FBI. They'll back us up if we need it, but we have to handle it.</p>
<p>(BRASS turns to leave. JACK GARRIS stops him.)</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: Uh, detective, wait. What are my chances here ... of-of Laura ... f-for Laura?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: It's a three-hour window.</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: Will somebody just tell me the truth?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: A ransom message with a short fuse means that the victim's situation will cause her to expire by or before the deadline regardless of what we do.</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: In a car trunk -- that kind of thing.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: It's a form of passive murder if you want to be, uh...<br />
'honest' about it.</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: Then you guys have got to move.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Mr. Garris ... it's my experience that, in situations like this if you want to go fast, go slow.</p>
<p>HARD CUT TO END OF TEASER ROLL TITLE CREDITS</p>
<p>(COMMERCIAL BREAK)</p>
<hr />
<p>FADE IN.</p>
<hr />
[INT. GARRIS RESIDENCE - BACK HALLWAY -- NIGHT]</p>
<p>(SARA walks cautiously into the hallway. She looks at the security pad on the wall.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Get a picture of the security pad. Someone touches it before it's dusted, I break their fingers. </p>
<p>(SARA turns her head and works her way down the hallway. The photographer/tech follows her.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Shoot the mirror, point of disturbance the lamp, point of disturbance ... </p>
<p>(SARA puts an evidence marker on the mirror and she puts evidence marker #2 on the lamp on the floor. The tech takes pictures of the security pad on the wall, then turns to take pictures of the mirror.)</p>
<p>(SARA looks up and sees something on the doorframe. As she stares at it, NICK appears behind her also staring at the doorframe. SARA turns around.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: ... you're standing in my crime scene.</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: No. (he holds up the disk) (beat) You're in mine.</p>
<p>(NICK smiles and turns to walk out of the house.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: (envious) You're doing audio? I wanted that.</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: I outrank you.</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Technicality. Who did Grissom handpick to work here?</p>
<p>(NICK chuckles dryly.)</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: Keep telling yourself that.</p>
<p>(SARA turns back to look at the doorframe)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: (to the tech) Get the lights, will you?</p>
<p><strong>Technician</strong>: Got it.</p>
<p>(The TECHNICIAN turns the lights off. SARA gets out her ALS and looks at the stuff on the doorframe.)</p>
<p>CUT TO:</p>
<hr />
[INT. GARRIS RESIDENCE - LIVING ROOM -- NIGHT]</p>
<p>(BRASS talks with JACK GARRIS.)</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: Shady friends? No. Laura surrounds herself with quality people-- people of real substance.</p>
<p>(BRASS nods.)</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Has her behavior changed recently -- drug use, alcohol, things like that?</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: (interrupts) No, no, no, nothing like that. </p>
<p>(BRASS doesn't say anything. He stands up.)</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: She's a health nut -- we both are.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Uh-huh.</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: Look, I'm a rich man. How do we let this guy know I'll pay?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: We don't want to go there yet.</p>
<p>CUT TO:</p>
<hr />
[EXT. GARRIS RESIDENCE â€“ SUV -- CURB -- NIGHT]</p>
<p>(NICK starts the car's engine. GRISSOM leans in through the open passenger window to give NICK last minute instructions.)</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Nick, tell lab to do a reverse algorithm on that tape and then get it into enhancement.</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: I know what to tell the audio guy.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: You've been a Level Three for what, two weeks?</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: I got it under control. </p>
<p>(GRISSOM stares at NICK and doesn't say anything. NICK smiles.)</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: Swear. </p>
<p>(GRISSOM moves away from the window.)</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: Later.</p>
<p>(NICK shifts the car into gear.)</p>
<p>CUT TO:</p>
<hr />
[INT. GARRIS RESIDENCE - BEDROOM]</p>
<p>(Camera opens on a broken picture frame on the floor next to evidence marker #6. A camera flashes.)</p>
<p>(GRISSOM walks into the bedroom. SARA is standing at the dresser taking photographs. GRISSOM walks in and looks around.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Cursory call -- looks like a professional job. </p>
<p>(GRISSOM doesn't say anything. He leans over to look at the various framed pictures on the tabletop. SARA continues to call it.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: (continuing) Our guy bypasses the security system, surprises the wife in the back hall. Drags her in here. She grabs onto the doorway -- sign of struggle. No sign of sexual assault. He's in, they're out. </p>
<p>(GRISSOM glances down and sees a smudge on the carpet. It grabs his attention and he leans in low to examine it closely.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: (continuing) Probably ... egress through those doors. Excuse me, is my evaluation interrupting you?</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: No, no, no. I barely heard you.</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Glad I have a healthy ego. You find something interesting there?</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Dirt.</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: You're ... so ... technical ... I can hardly keep up, but ...</p>
<p>GRISSOM; Oh, sorry, but, uh ... out of context, it's ... just dirt.</p>
<p>CUT TO:</p>
<hr />
[EXT. GARRIS RESIDENCE - BACKYARD - NIGHT -- CONTINUOUS]</p>
<p>(GRISSOM steps through the open balcony doors and out into the backyard patio. SARA follows him.)</p>
<p>(He looks around. SARA sniffs.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Did you just slap on bad cologne?</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: I never wear it. It interferes with the job.</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: It's almost sweet.</p>
<p>(GRISSOM sees something.)</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Hmm.</p>
<p>(He finds a cloth on the ground. He picks it up and smells it. He offers it to SARA to smell.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Can't be chloroform.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Halothane, maybe.</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: We'll confirm it in GC mass spec.</p>
<p>(SARA reaches into her bag for an evidence bag.)</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: &quot;Looks like a professional job,&quot; I think you said. </p>
<p>(SARA glances up at GRISSOM and smiles.)</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Care to amend your evaluation? I mean, if the guy forgets the rag he used to knock her out, he can't be much of a pro.</p>
<p>(He puts the cloth in the evidence bag SARA holds open for him.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: (smiles) I keep trying to be your star pupil.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Sara, that was a seminar. This is real. Pebbles, tile -- the front is all concrete.</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: No dirt. Context-- there is dirt on the carpeting inside.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: In an otherwise spotless house.</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: You're saying kidnapper tracked the dirt in.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Possible. As of now, that's about all we have, so ... I guess we follow the dirt.</p>
<p>(GRISSOM walks past SARA and back into the house. SARA looks up, shakes her head and smiles.)</p>
<p>CUT TO:</p>
<hr />
[EXT. RESIDENTIAL STREET -- NIGHT]</p>
<p>(Cameras flash. The female in the body bag is covered and the bag zipped up.)</p>
<p>(Cut to: The ambulance door closes and the vehicle drives off.)</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: How many hit-and-runs we had this year?</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: Too many. One thing I can't stand is a punk coward.</p>
<p>(CATHERINE looks down at the roadside and stares at the scooter that the little girl was riding. It's on its side next to evidence marker #1 and a ruler.)</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: My daughter wants one of these scooters. Says she's the only kid in the world who doesn't have one.</p>
<p>(CATHERINE steps up to the fallen scooter and kneels down next to it. WARRICK looks at CATHERINE'S back.)</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: You want me to take this case alone?</p>
<p>(CATHERINE turns around to look back up at WARRICK.)</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: You feeling all right, Warrick?</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: Oh, I'm fine. I'm ... I'm looking after you. (He shakes his head.)<br />
It's that thing with Holly Gribbs. </p>
<p>(CATHERINE stands up and looks at WARRICK.)</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: It's made me think of who I am to my partners, you know?</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: So this is what-- your new leaf?</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: (embarrassed, yet serious) You keep on busting on me, you won't see any kind of leaf.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: (smiles) Hey, relax. I'll play nice. Thank you for the offer. I'm okay. I can handle this one. </p>
<p>(CATHERINE turns back to look at the scooter. She kneels down next to it.)</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: It'd be nice if this paint transfer had just one special property.<br />
(beat) You want to call it?</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: (points) Vehicle's humming down Rochester. The vic was on her scooter, heading eastbound. Car brakes here ...</p>
<p>(WARRICK points to the tire marks on the road next to evidence marker #3.)</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: ... impact here. The vic was thrown -- what-- twenty meters?</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: And all we've got is some paint that's going to match up to about 20 million other vehicles.</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: (sighs) Yeah.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: b*st*rd.</p>
<p>CUT TO:</p>
<hr />
[INT. GARRIS RESIDENCE - FOYER -- NIGHT]</p>
<p>(JACK GARRIS heads out the front door. He turns around to look at BRASS.)</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: Are you ordering me not to pay the ransom?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: It's a very strong suggestion, yes.</p>
<p>(Behind BRASS, GRISSOM turns the corner and heads slowly toward the men.)</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: After you told me she could already be dead? I don't believe you guys. This is my wife. I'm paying the ransom.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Look, if you give up the cash the kidnapper has no reason to communicate with us.</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: Does anyone on your team know where Laura is right now?</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: We have an entire forensics lab working on this.</p>
<p><strong>Jack Garris</strong>: Yeah? Something tells me I'll see her before you do.</p>
<p>(And with that, JACK GARRIS walks out of the house with the money in a briefcase.)</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Well, your job just got harder.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Yeah.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: (sighs) I'll stay close to the money.</p>
<p>(BRASS heads out the front door to follow JACK GARRIS.)</p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[SCOPE VIEW OF THE DIRT]</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: (V.O.) This dirt has interesting properties.</p>
<hr />
[INT. CSI - LAB -- NIGHT]</p>
<p>(SARA and GRISSOM are in the lab. SARA is looking through the scope at the dirt.)</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: The gold flecks -- that's easy -- it's gold. The little gray squiggles? Cyanide.</p>
<p>(She looks up from the scope.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Cyanide? He poisoned her?</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: No. Did you know that Nevada produces 80% of the country's gold? Miners drop cyanide powder into the dirt and it draws the gold to the surface.</p>

<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: I know it's not fair. Life isn't fair.</p>
<p>CUT TO:</p>
<hr />
[INT. POLICE DEPARTMENT - CORRIDOR -- DAY] </p>
<p>(JAMES MOORE empties his pockets. His grandfather stands on the side watching him.)</p>
<p><strong>James Moore</strong>: Don't worry, Pops. I'll be okay.</p>
<p>(CHARLES MOORE sighs.)</p>
<p><strong>Charles Moore</strong>: My grandson going to jail is never okay. (beat) You stay alive in there. You hear me?</p>
<p><strong>James Moore</strong>: (nods) You, too. Don't be going downhill.</p>
<p>(They hug each other.)</p>
<p>(WARRICK swallows. The OFFICER step up to get JAMES. The cell door opens. WARRICK grabs a pen and stops him.)</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: Hold on. James ... the first couple days are going to be the toughest. Here. </p>
<p>(On JAMES' hand, WARRICK writes down a phone number.)</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: Here's my cell number. You run into any problems you call me. I'll be right there. All right? Keep your head up.</p>
<p>(The officer leads JAMES to his cell. The doors close behind him.)</p>
<p>(WARRICK heads out, past CHARLES MOORE who had watched the entire exchange.. JAMES sits down in his cell.)</p>
<p>CUT TO:</p>
<hr />
[INT. POLICE DEPARTMENT - HALLWAY - DAY -- CONTINUOUS]</p>
<p>(WARRICK walks out of the room and into the hallway.)</p>
<p><strong>Charles Moore</strong>: (o.s.) You didn't have to do that - </p>
<p>(WARRICK turns around to look at CHARLES MOORE.)</p>
<p><strong>Charles Moore</strong>: ... give James your number. I've seen other law enforcement people in my day ...</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: &quot;My day.&quot; (chuckles) Reminds me of my grandmother. Yeah, I gave her a lot of rough nights, too. She used to call me her &quot;work in progress.&quot;</p>
<p><strong>Charles Moore</strong>: Yeah. Well ... she did something right.</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: (nods slightly) Maybe.</p>
<p>(WARRICK turns and walks away.)</p>
<p>FADE TO BLACK.</p>
<p><strong>End</strong></p>
