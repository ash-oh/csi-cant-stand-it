<p>[EXT. VARIOUS LAS VEGAS CITY (STOCK) -- NIGHT] </p>
<p><strong>Operator</strong>: (V.O.) 911. What is your emergency?</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: (V.O.) (panicked) Yes. There's been an accident. An accident. Please ... </p>
<p>[EXT. - NIGHT]</p>
<p><strong>Operator</strong>: (V.O.) What kind of emergency, sir?</p>
<p>(A paramedic takes his kit from the vehicle.) </p>
<p>[EXT. VARIOUS LAS VEGAS CITY (STOCK) -- NIGHT] </p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: (V.O.) Please, ma'am, I need you to send an ambulance. Please, just hurry.</p>
<p>[EXT. LESTER RESIDENCE - FRONT DRIVE - NIGHT]</p>
<p><strong>Operator</strong>: Calm down, sir, paramedics are on their way.</p>
<p>(The paramedics head for the house. A man with blood on his shirt runs out of the house.) </p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: Oh, my God! Please, you've got to help me! Please, hurry!</p>
<p><strong>Paramedic</strong>: Sir, where are you injured?</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: What the hell? No, not me, my wife!</p>
<p>(He turns and leads them to his house.)</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: Hurry! She's in here!</p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[INT. LESTER RESIDENCE - NIGHT - CONTINUOUS]</p>
<p>(He leads them into the darkened house to show them the woman on the stairs in a pool of blood.) </p>
<p><strong>Paramedic 1</strong>: Get out of the way, sir.</p>
<p>(Paramedic 1 rushes over to the woman. He steps in the pool of blood. He turns and looks over at his partner.) </p>
<p><strong>Paramedic 1</strong>: De-fib.</p>
<p><strong>Paramedic 2</strong>: Yeah.</p>
<p>(The paramedic places the defibrillator on the ground. Ray Lester starts panicking.) </p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: What does that mean?</p>
<p><strong>Paramedic</strong>: Sir, sir!</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: Wh-What does that mean?</p>
<p><strong>Paramedic</strong>: Sir, please stand back. (to the other paramedic) Go ahead. Go ahead.</p>
<p>(He puts the electrodes on her and turns on the machine. There's a flatline and steady tone.) </p>
<p><strong>Paramedic</strong>: No response.</p>
<p>WHITE FLASH TO: </p>
<p>[EXT. LESTER RESIDENCE - FRONT WALK - NIGHT]</p>
<p>(Catherine and Grissom, both carrying their kits, duck under the crime scene tape to meet up with Brass and the two paramedics.) </p>
<p>(Brass turns around and immediately starts filling them in.) </p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: There was an emergency call-out; the paramedics responded. They found Mrs. Lester on the back stairs. They pronounced at 12:16 a.m.</p>
<p>(Grissom notes the paramedics' bloodied shoes.) </p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: And, of course, you walked through the house.</p>
<p><strong>Paramedic</strong>: We were responding to a traumatic injury. Our priority was to assist the victim.</p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[INT. LESTER RESIDENCE - NIGHT - CONTINUOUS]</p>
<p>(Brass leads Catherine and Grissom into the house. There are bloodied footprint tracks on the floor. Over in the living room, Ray Lester is sitting in a chair, his legs, shirt and hands bloodied.) </p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: That's Ray Lester, the vic's husband.</p>
<p>(Brass continues into the house to lead them to the dead body.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Was he injured?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: No, uh, that's his wife's blood. He said he checked to see if she was still alive and he held her until the paramedics came.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Wouldn't you?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: You never met my ex.</p>
<p>[INT. LESTER RESIDENCE - KITCHEN - NIGHT - CONTINUOUS]</p>
<p>(They enter the kitchen and find a bottle of tequila, less than a quarter full, and two empty shot glasses on the counter.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Tequila will always remind me of Se√±or Frog's.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Which one?</p>
<p>(Grissom looks outside </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Cancun. My honeymoon. My dime.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Well, if you ever want to go back, it's on me.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Is that a proposal?</p>
<p>(They continue to follow the bloodied footprints through the kitchen and on into the next room where the body is.) </p>
<p><strong>David Phillips</strong>: It's like the Red Sea in here.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: The Red Sea's not red, David.</p>
<p><strong>David Phillips</strong>: No, it's blue from afar and transparent when held in hand, like any other body of water. I was just speaking figuratively.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: I'm assuming those boot prints aren't yours.</p>
<p><strong>David Phillips</strong>: Please, I was just waiting for you guys to clear a path. Blame the paramedics.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: This girl's head and spine are perfectly aligned.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Yeah, when you fall down the stairs, your head tends to go in one direction, your body in another.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: I'll take a statement from the husband and the paramedics; see if she was moved.</p>
<p>(Grissom looks at the door.) </p>
<p><strong>David Phillips</strong>: You know, twice as many people die each year from falling down stairs than from accidental gunfire?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: You didn't just get back from one of those coroner's conferences, did you?</p>
<p>(Grissom looks at Catherine, who glances at him and sees his look.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: What are you thinking?</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Led Zeppelin, &quot;Stairway to Heaven.&quot;</p>
<p>FADE TO END OF TEASER ROLL TITLE CREDITS</p>
<p>(COMMERCIAL BREAK)</p>
<hr />
<p>FADE IN.</p>
<p>[EXT. LESTER RESIDENCE - FRONT DRIVE -- DAY] </p>
<p>(The coroner's van is parked in the driveway. Officer keeps the crowd behind the taped-off area. One of the paramedics puts his shoes into the paper bag Greg holds for him. The other paramedic is doing the same.) </p>
<p>(Sara parks the car. Warrick, Nick and Sara exit the car and walk over to Greg.) </p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: Hey, Sanders.</p>
<p><strong>Greg</strong>: Hey. Back stairs. And, uh, you might want to hug the wall.</p>
<p>(They turn and head for the house as Greg bags the other paramedic's shoes. He glances in the bag and takes a whiff of it.) </p>
<p><strong>Greg</strong>: Whew! Did you ever hear of Odor Eaters? Never mind.</p>
<p><strong>Paramedic 1</strong>: We don't get another boot allowance till spring.</p>
<p><strong>Greg</strong>: Well, I'll try to get 'em back to you before your next shift, okay? Uh, one more question: Did either of you guys move the body?</p>
<p><strong>Paramedic 2</strong>: Didn't have to. She was face-up. </p>
<p><strong>Paramedic 1</strong>: I unbuttoned her blouse to apply the leads, but that's it.</p>
<p><strong>Paramedic 2</strong>: We tried not to touch anything. Once we saw that blood, we knew you'd be showing up.</p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[INT. LESTER RESIDENCE - DAY -- CONTINUOUS]</p>
<p>(Everyone stands at the base of the stairs looking down at Becky Lester's body as Grissom preps them.) </p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Becky Lester. Catherine and I got here a half an hour after her husband called 911.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Edges of the blood pools were already dry. The pools themselves were tacky.</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: Which means she'd been down here at least an hour or two before you guys even got here.</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Why did the husband wait so long to call?</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Good question.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Coroner won't touch the body till Catherine and I are done processing around it, so, Sara, would you please go up the front stairs and work your way down the back stairs?</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: You got it.</p>
<p>(Sara picks up her kit and leaves.) </p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Warrick, could you process the blood evidence from the front door to the back stairwell? And if you'll notice, she doesn't have any blood on her feet.</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: So I can assume those bloody footprints aren't hers.</p>
<p>(Warrick turns and leaves.) </p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Nick, Brass is in the study with the husband and he's waiting for you.</p>
<p>(Nick picks up his kit and leaves.) </p>
<p>(In the kitchen, Warrick puts an evidence marker down on the floor and snaps a photo of the bloodied footprints.) </p>
<p>(He continues to process the scene.)</p>
<p>(Various dissolves of Warrick putting down more evidence markers and taking more pictures as he works his way to the door.) </p>
<p>(Off on the counter, he notices a single blood drop. He snaps a photo of it. He puts a plastic protective covering over it, then looks around in the trash. He finds a napkin with blood on it. He picks it up.) </p>
<p>(On the second floor, Sara examines the stairs, starting from the top, and works her way down.) </p>
<p>(Grissom puts a measuring ruler near some blood drops and snaps a photo of it.</p>
<p>(Catherine takes a swab of the blood pool.)</p>
<p>(Sara prints the side of the stairway wall.) </p>
<p>(Catherine measures the blood spatter on the wall near the body.) </p>
<p>(Grissom is snapping photos of the contents in the hallway closet nearby.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Hey, Grissom, spatter on the wall doesn't exceed 18 inches.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Well, that's consistent with the fall.</p>
<p>(Quick flash of: Becky Lester appears at the top of the stairs and trips. She falls down the stairs. Blood spatters on the wall near the bottom.)</p>
<p>(End of flash.) </p>
<p>(Sara is dusting the stairway walls.) </p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Guys, if she fell, even if she was pushed down these stairs, I would expect to find smudge marks on these walls.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Yeah, she would've instinctively reached out for leverage.</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: There are none. Not to mention, that if she was up here before she was down there ... she should've landed head first.</p>
<p>(Quick flash of: Becky Lester trips and falls. She lands head on the bottom of the steps. End of flash.) </p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: The body position is inconsistent with the fall.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Well, it's inconsistent if she fell down the stairs. Maybe she fell up.</p>
<p>(Quick flash of: Becky Lester is walking up the stairs. She trips and falls, hitting her head on the stairs. She then falls a couple steps down. She gets up long enough to flip over, then falls back on the steps. End of flash.)</p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Catherine, can I borrow your micrometer?</p>
<p>(Catherine hands Sara the micrometer, which Sara uses to measure one of the blood drops on the wall.) </p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Blood spatter measures two millimeters.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Medium spatter. Suggests impact with a blunt object.</p>
<p>(Quick flash of: Becky Lester is being beaten with an object. End of flash.) </p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: But if she was hit, where's the castoff?</p>
<p>(Grissom looks at the ceiling above them.) </p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: It is a low ceiling. Pretty hard to swing a weapon.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Sometimes first blush gives you a head rush.</p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[INT. LESTER RESIDENCE - SITTING ROOM - DAY -- CONTINUOUS] </p>
<p>(Brass interviews Ray Lester while Nick gathers evidence from him.) </p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: I don't think I moved her. Um, I mean, I cradled her head in my lap. Tried to wake her up, but that's it.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: So, you and your wife went to Piero's for dinner, got home around 10:00, got out the tequila. Then what?</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: I, you know, got comfortable ... then we went outside and did a few shots.</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: How'd the tequila get back inside?</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: Um, the phone rang and it was my daughter Susan.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Your daughter?</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: Yeah, from my first marriage. She's, she's staying at Becky's dad's.</p>
<p>(Quick flash of: Out the patio, the phone rings; Ray Lester answers it.) </p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: (to phone) All right. It's Susan.</p>
<p><strong>Becky Lester</strong>: Oh. (to phone) Hey, babe. Uh-huh. </p>
<p>(Ray pours himself a drink.) </p>
<p><strong>Becky Lester</strong>: (to phone) Okay. (to Ray) It's boy trouble. I'll take this inside.</p>
<p>(She presses the hold button on the phone, stands up and heads inside.) </p>
<p>(End of flash. Resume to present.) </p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: What did you do after that?</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: Another shot or two and then I guess I fell asleep. And I woke up around midnight. (Nick looks up from what he's doing.) And I figured that she and Susan had just gotten into one of their talk-a-thons.</p>
<p>(Quick flash of: Ray goes back into the house and puts the tequila and glasses on the counter.) </p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: (V.O.) I took the tequila and the glasses into the kitchen and that's when I saw her.</p>
<p>(He turns and sees Becky on the stairs.) </p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: Oh, my God. Oh, please!</p>
<p>(He runs over to her and holds her.) </p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: Oh, please, please! Oh, God. Oh, please, Oh please.</p>
<p>(He puts her back down, his hands in her blood.) </p>
<p>(Flash to: He runs to the phone and dials, the blood from his hands all over the phone.) </p>
<p>(End of flash. Resume to present.) </p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Now, is there any reason you can think of why your wife would be using the back stairs?</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: From the kitchen, it's closer to the bedroom.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: You never mentioned anything about the bedroom.</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: Are you trying to trip me up or something? I mean, I don't ... maybe she went to, uh, brush her teeth or put on her nightgown. I really don't know.</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: Okay, sir. I'm going to need to take your clothing.</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: I'll go upstairs and change.</p>
<p>(Ray stands up; Nick stops him.) </p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: No, that's okay. We're still processing upstairs. You can remove them right here and I'll give you a jumpsuit.</p>
<p>(He turns and looks at Brass.) </p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: I'll close my eyes, if you're shy.</p>
<p>(Ray removes his shirt to show teeth marks on his chest.) </p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: Those don't look like mosquito bites.</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: My wife likes to bite, and I don't mind it, okay?</p>
<p><strong>Nick</strong>: (nods) Okay.</p>
<p>(Nick puts the shirt in a bag.) </p>
<p>(The coroners wheel out the body on a gurney. Ray's eyes follow the gurney as it passes the sitting room doorway on its way out of the house. He appears very upset.) </p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[INT. LESTER RESIDENCE - BACK ROOM - DAY -- CONTINUOUS] </p>
<p>(Brass walks back into the room where Grissom and Catherine are working.) </p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Okay, the husband says he fell asleep, he woke up his wife was dead.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: He's a good sleeper.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Yeah, I'm sure he's gonna say something like that. Anyway, there's a daughter. She spent the night at her grandfather's, who lives a few blocks away. I'm gonna check it out.</p>
<p>(Brass leaves.) </p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: I'm ready.</p>
<p>(Grissom and Catherine head for the kitchen where Warrick is.) </p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: The blue markers denote the footprints. They're all consistent with the husband.</p>
<p>(Quick visualization of: Ray is near Becky's body. He's already stepped in the pool of blood. He runs over to the phone, leaving bloodied footprints behind him.) </p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: The footprints are associated with gravitational blood drops.</p>
<p>(The visualization continues: Ray puts the phone down and runs back to the body. The wet blood from his hands drops to the wooden floor.) </p>
<p>(Warrick points to the other markers.) </p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: The yellow markers denote the paramedics' boot prints.</p>
<p>(Quick visualization of: The two paramedics are at the body. They stand up and head back out, leaving a trail of bloody prints on the floor. Visualization ends.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: And what about this here, on the counter?</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: It's a single blood drop. </p>
<p>(Quick flash of: A single blood drop falls and hits the counter.) </p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: I'm thinking that's from when the husband reached for the phone.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: That's a good observation. What do you got in the bag?</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: This ...</p>
<p>(Warrick opens the bag and takes out a paper towel.) </p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: ... is a bloody paper towel I found in the trash. It's too textured to print, but Sanders talked to the paramedics, who confirmed that it wasn't theirs. I'm thinking it was from the husband when he tried to clean up and saw it wouldn't be too easy.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Lucky for us.</p>

<p><strong>Robbins</strong>: This is a blood clot. Take a look.</p>
<p>(Robbins pries the brain apart to show Warrick what it looks like inside.) </p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: Huh. The blood's continuous. </p>
<p><strong>Robbins</strong>: Comes from deep within the parenchyma. The first wife had a stroke and it was fatal.</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: What about the scalp lacerations?</p>
<p><strong>Robbins</strong>: Perimortem.</p>
<p><strong>Warrick</strong>: So she must've hit her head on the stairs on the way down.</p>
<p><strong>Robbins</strong>: Well, I can't tell you about the second wife, but this was a natural.</p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[INT. CSI - LAB -- NIGHT] </p>
<p>(Greg runs an ALS over the blackmail note. He finds a watermark on the bottom.) </p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: That's the Western Airlines logo.</p>
<p><strong>Greg</strong>: Over the past few years, corporations have begun embedding their insignia into the stationery for security purposes.</p>
<p>(Catherine's phone rings; she answers it.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: If Becky wrote the note, she wrote it at work. (to phone) This is Catherine. Oh, yeah. I'm on my way.</p>
<p>(Catherine leaves the lab.) </p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[INT. CSI - LAB] </p>
<p>(Grissom meets with Ecklie and ADA Jeffrey Sinclair.) </p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: For the record, I think you're filing prematurely.</p>
<p><strong>ADA Jeffrey Sinclair</strong>: As always, I value your opinion. But I'm confident in our decision to move forward. But, come on, Grissom, granted the first death was a natural -- but two dead women married to the same guy found in a stairwell? Lightning doesn't strike in the same place twice.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Actually, it does. The Empire State Building gets struck twenty times a year. </p>
<p>(Sinclair looks at Ecklie, who rolls his eyes.) </p>
<p><strong>Conrad Ecklie</strong>: Let's just run the case.</p>
<p>(Catherine rushes into the lab.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Sorry, I got held up.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: No, you're just in time. Ms. Willows, would you bring Mr. Sinclair up to speed?</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: I'd be happy to. Ray and Becky Lester were unhappily married. She was having an affair with her boss; he was indulging his biting fetish. They were having financial problems. But Becky believed that Ray was hiding money. Two weeks ago, Ray received a blackmail note demanding 200 grand or the wife finds out about bite girl. Well, we have just confirmed that the blackmailer and the wife are one and the same.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Based on what?</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: The envelope of the ransom note. DNA was a match.</p>
<p><strong>ADA Jeffrey Sinclair</strong>: The wife was blackmailing the husband?</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: (chuckles) Never underestimate the deviousness of a malcontented spouse.</p>
<p><strong>ADA Jeffrey Sinclair</strong>: Well, he found out and he killed her. </p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: We don't have any evidence to support that.</p>
<p><strong>ADA Jeffrey Sinclair</strong>: Run the timeline.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Couple got home around 10:00 P.M. They went outside and had some tequila. At 10:08, Ray's daughter from his first marriage phoned. Becky went inside to take the call. </p>
<p><strong>ADA Jeffrey Sinclair</strong>: How long did they talk?</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: It's not in Brass's notes.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Well, I have the phone records here, so ... uh, the call lasted ... two minutes?</p>
<p><strong>Conrad Ecklie</strong>: I thought the husband told Nick it was a marathon call.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Yeah, I'm just seeing this for the first time here. Um ... the incoming call did not come from the grandfather's house.</p>
<p>(Catherine turns her phone on.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: I'm gonna call this number.</p>
<p>(The phone rings. An answering machine goes on.) </p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: (answering machine) You've reached the home office of Ray Lester. Please leave your details after the beep.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Susan called her stepmother from inside the house.</p>
<p><strong>Conrad Ecklie</strong>: Did anybody process that office phone?</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: The office was away from the crime scene. We, uh, checked it for a murder weapon, but that's about it. I'll get an investigator over there. Unless you're in too much of a hurry.</p>
<p>(Grissom leaves.) </p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[INT. LESTER RESIDENCE -- DAY] </p>
<p>(Greg and Sara enter the house. They look over at the desk and see the phone.) </p>
<p><strong>Greg</strong>: There it is.</p>
<p>(Greg snaps a photo of the phone. Sara picks the phone up and checks it. She flips it over and finds the blood on the back.) </p>
<p>(Camera zooms in for a close-up.) </p>
<p><strong>Sara</strong>: Greg ... there's a fingerprint in blood.</p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[EXT./INT. STEIN RESIDENCE -- DAY] </p>
<p>(Brass knocks on the door. Grandpa Stein answers it. Catherine and Brass are on the front porch.) </p>
<p><strong>Grandpa Stein</strong>:Detective, what's going on?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Mr. Stein, where's Susan?</p>
<p>(Susan steps out from the back.) </p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: Is everything okay with Dad?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: We have a warrant to search your house.</p>
<p>(Catherine slips inside the doorway.) </p>
<p><strong>Grandpa Stein</strong>:A warrant?! What?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Would you both please step outside with this officer?</p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: Can I get my homework?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Well, uh ... </p>
<p>(Catherine turns and looks at Brass.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Sure. That'd be all right.</p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: Okay.</p>
<p>(Susan hurries to her room. Catherine follows her.) </p>
<p>(In the bedroom, Susan is closing her backpack. She turns around just as Catherine steps in the doorway.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: I'm gonna need to see inside your backpack.</p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: It's just my books and stuff.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: I'd like to see what you're studying.</p>
<p>(Catherine takes the backpack from her. She puts it on the bed and opens it. Inside the backpack, Catherine finds books ... and a bloodied shirt.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: What did you hit her with?</p>
<p>(Susan looks around. Catherine looks at the closet door.) </p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: I want to talk to my dad.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Why don't we take a ride downtown. You can call him from there.</p>
<p>(Susan leaves.) </p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: (to Catherine) Nice work.</p>
<p>(Catherine puts the shirt aside. She picks up the camera and opens the closet door. She takes a photo of the shirts hanging inside.) </p>
<p>(She takes out her flashlight and looks around. She pushes the clothes aside and finds a cane leaning against the wall. She looks at the cane handle and puts it on the doorknob. She takes a swab and tests for blood. The swab turns pink.) </p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[INT. POLICE DEPARTMENT - INTERVIEW ROOM -- DAY] </p>
<p>(Brass interviews Susan Lester. Ms. Karpell sits next to her.) </p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: My stepmom was a bitch.</p>
<p><strong>Ms. Karpell</strong>: Susan, you're not helping. Please, stay quiet.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Excuse me for a minute.</p>
<p>(In the observation room, Grissom and Catherine watch the interview.) </p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: You know, I guess this could explain why Becky's skull was lacerated and not fractured.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: She wasn't strong enough.</p>
<p>[INT. POLICE DEPARTMENT - INTERVIEW ROOM -- DAY] </p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: I had to protect him.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Protect who, Susan?</p>
<p><strong>Ms. Karpell</strong>: Susan!</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Protect your father? Protect your father from what?</p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: She was gonna leave him.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: So that's why you killed your stepmother--to protect your father.</p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: Last week, I skipped soccer practice and came home early.</p>
<p>(Quick flash of: Susan rushes up the stairs and puts her backpack in her room. She hears Becky and a man laughing.) </p>
<p><strong>Becky Lester</strong>: You are so damn brilliant.</p>
<p><strong>Adam Gilford</strong>: I just found the digital video and printed out the stills.</p>
<p>(Susan heads for Becky's bedroom and finds Becky in bed with Adam Gilford.) </p>
<p><strong>Becky Lester</strong>: &quot;Dental records.&quot; I married a moron.</p>
<p>(She sees them kiss.)</p>
<p><strong>Adam Gilford</strong>: Did you check the P.O. Box today?</p>
<p><strong>Becky Lester</strong>: Empty, but he's got another week. Ray's whipped. He brought up counseling again. The idiot would die before letting me in on his freak-ass fetish.</p>
<p>(End of flashback. Resume to present.) </p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: My dad only married Becky because of me.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Explain that to me.</p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: Well, he thought I needed a mom and that's why he stayed with her.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Blaming herself for the marriage.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: She doesn't seem to blame herself for ending it.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: So you waited for your granddad to go to sleep and you snuck out of the house, snuck back into your house then you called your stepmom from your dad's telephone line. Pretty tricky. And then, you must've ... hid somewhere.</p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: I hid in the closet. I told her we had to talk and that I knew about her boyfriend.</p>
<p>(Quick flash of: Susan is in the closet and on the phone.) </p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: (to phone) Uh, hey, Dad. Is Becky there? Could I talk to her? </p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: (to phone) All right. </p>
<p>(He hands the phone to Becky.) </p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: (to Becky) It's Susan.</p>
<p><strong>Becky Lester</strong>: (to phone) Hey, babe.</p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: (to phone) Just tell Dad you'll take the call inside and meet me in my room.</p>
<p><strong>Becky Lester</strong>: (to phone) Okay. (to Ray) It's boy trouble. (she puts the call on hold) I'll take this inside.</p>
<p>(Flash to: Becky heads inside. As soon as she passes the closet and heads upstairs, Susan rushes out and uses the cane to hit her over the head.) </p>
<p>(Becky hits her head on the stairs. She gets up and turns around. Susan hits her again. She falls backward. Susan hits her again, blood spatters on the wall.) </p>
<p>(She looks at the blood on the cane handle and on her shirt.) </p>
<p>(End of flashback. Resume to present.) </p>
<p><strong>Ms. Karpell</strong>: Are we done here?</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Almost. We're almost done. You know, Susan, there was a bloody paper towel left in the trash. Can you tell me about that?</p>
<p>(Quick flashback to: Susan runs to the kitchen and grabs a paper towel to wipe the blood off the cane handle.) </p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: (V.O.) I went into the kitchen. Tried to clean up.</p>
<p>(She tosses the paper towel in the trash.)</p>
<p>(End of flashback.)</p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: I put the phone back in my dad's office and I walked back to Grandpa's.</p>
<p><strong>Brass</strong>: Where was your father when you killed your stepmother?</p>
<p><strong>Susan Lester</strong>: He was outside. Asleep.</p>
<p>(In the observation room, Grissom and Catherine listen.) </p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Did you catch that?</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Timeline is off.</p>
<p><strong>Grissom</strong>: Curious. You want to talk to the dad and I'll call the DA?</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Sure.</p>
<p>(Grissom leaves.) </p>
<p>CUT TO:<br />
[INT. POLICE DEPARTMENT - WAITING ROOM]</p>
<p>(Ray Lester waits in the waiting room with an officer. Catherine walks in.) </p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: You knew.</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: What?</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Your daughter killed your wife and you knew it.</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: I don't know what you're talking about.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Susan attacked Becky as soon as she went into the house. But you admitted to having another drink. You would've heard the attack.</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: I'm sorry, but that's what happ ...</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: You went into the house and found Susan wiping blood off of her hands. You sent her back to her grandfather's. You came up with a story to tell the cops, and then called 911. You were protecting your daughter. And now she's protecting you.</p>
<p><strong>Ray Lester</strong>: You can't prove it.</p>
<p><strong>Catherine</strong>: Well, either way, you have to live with it.</p>
<p>(Catherine turns and leaves. She walks down the hallway. Susan Lester, Ms. Karpell and an officer exit the interview room. Catherine walks past them.) </p>
<p>FADE TO BLACK.</p>
<p><strong>End</strong></p>
